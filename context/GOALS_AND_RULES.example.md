## Investment Philosophy

- **Core + satellite:** 80-90% of equity allocation in broad index funds. 10-20% in high-conviction individual stocks with asymmetric upside. The core is Bogle. The satellite is where I express views.
- **On high-conviction picks:** I believe in having a few bets with high convexity. These are positions where the downside is bounded but the upside is multiples. I size on entry, not on current value. If a position appreciates into a large dollar amount, that's the thesis working -- I don't trim just because it got big.
- **On options as income/entry strategy:** I write options on high-conviction names I already own or want to own. Short covered calls on positions I'm comfortable trimming at a target price. Short puts on names I want to add at lower prices. This generates income and creates disciplined entry/exit points. I'm not speculating on options -- I'm using them as a tool for managing positions I already have conviction on.
- **On timing:** I'm an opportunistic buyer. Big down days (3-4%+ market drops) are buying opportunities. I'd rather deploy cash into a sell-off than into a market that ripped 4% yesterday. This isn't day-trading; it's using volatility as an entry signal.
- **On fixed income:** Bonds are a small allocation, not the load-bearing piece. T-bills are a parking lot for cash I haven't deployed yet, not a target allocation.
- **On diversification:** International equity exposure is the most common gap. Deploying T-bill maturities into VXUS is the path to close it.
- **On complexity:** I prefer simple instruments for the core. Direct indexing is the most complex strategy I run, and I let the platform handle it.
- **On crypto:** Speculative satellite position. Not a core holding. Don't add, don't panic-sell.
- **On private investments:** Sunk cost / long-duration optionality. Don't count on them for allocation purposes.

## Behavioral Patterns (be honest with me)

- **Freezing during drawdowns:** When markets drop 10%+, I know I should buy but I don't. The rules below are designed to force action.
- **T-bill overhang:** Cash in T-bills is dead weight. Each maturity is a deployment decision I tend to defer by just rolling into new T-bills. The system should default to "deploy" not "roll."
- **Analysis paralysis on deployment:** I think about deploying cash, research it, then don't act. The system should reduce the decision surface: "Your rules say X. Do you want to execute?"
- **Good at holding:** Once I own something, I don't panic-sell. The risk is inaction, not overreaction.

## Mechanical Rules

These are pre-committed actions. The system should flag when a rule is triggered and ask if I want to execute, not re-debate whether the rule is right.

### Cash Deployment Triggers
1. **10% drawdown rule:** When S&P 500 drops 10% from its all-time high, deploy 10% of deployable reserves (money market + near-term T-bill maturities) into equities.
2. **20% drawdown rule:** At 20% from ATH, deploy an additional 20% of remaining deployable reserves.
3. **T-bill maturity deployment:** When T-bills mature, default action is to deploy into equities per target allocation unless I explicitly choose to reinvest in T-bills. Each maturity is a forcing function.
4. **Monthly DCA floor:** Regardless of market conditions, deploy at least [floor amount] per month from cash into equities until cash allocation is within target band.

### Rebalancing Triggers
5. **5% drift threshold:** If any asset class drifts more than 5 percentage points from its target midpoint, flag for rebalancing.
6. **Annual rebalancing:** Full portfolio review in January regardless of drift.

### Do-Not Rules
7. **No chasing rallies:** Don't deploy cash the day after a big up move (3%+). Let it settle. Urgency after green days is FOMO, not strategy.
8. **No crypto additions:** Don't add to crypto positions. Hold or sell only.
9. **No selling during drawdowns:** Unless there's a tax-loss harvesting opportunity, do not sell equities when markets are down.

### Buy-the-Dip Rule
10. **Big down day trigger:** When S&P 500 drops 3%+ in a single day, deploy a portion of available cash into equities. Split 80-90% index (VTI/VXUS) and 10-20% into high-conviction picks if there's a name on the watchlist. This is a feature, not a bug. Volatility is the entry signal.
